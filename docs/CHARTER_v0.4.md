# THE FIRST NIGHT — project charter v0.4

## Premise

A castaway regains control on a tropical beach inside the Bermuda Triangle as
dusk closes in. The first need is credible survival. The island becomes strange
only after the player understands its ordinary dangers and routines.

## Player promise

Survive → operate → escape.

The player learns by doing: reading the terrain, handling physical objects,
crafting tools, building working structures, operating systems, and eventually
finding a way off the island. Knowledge is embodied and earned rather than
unlocked through abstract menus.

## Experience Constitution

1. The world is the primary interface.
2. Every consequence is readable and causally explainable.
3. Grounded survival earns later mystery.
4. Work performed by the player persists.
5. Offline progression is fair, bounded, deterministic, and never lethal.
6. The campfire is an emotional and mechanical sanctuary.
7. Authored places come before procedural abundance.
8. A small working system is better than a broad fake system.
9. Accessibility and motion comfort are baseline design inputs.
10. Tests protect player trust, especially around time and saves.

## Production position

The production game is first-person 3D in Godot, Android-first, with desktop and
browser test builds. The Phaser prototype is retained only as a behavioral
reference for time, warmth, fire, saves, reconciliation, and reports.

## Vertical slice 0.1

One authored beach and treeline at dusk. The player gathers loose materials,
crafts a primitive stone tool, harvests nodes, constructs a small modular
shelter and campfire, survives the night, saves, returns, and receives an honest
morning report. A single wreck clue opens the long horizon: escape.

## Non-goals

No multiplayer, survivors, enemies, hunting, combat, procedural islands,
vehicles, diving, caves, full progression tree, final art, monetization, store
submission, or elaborate narrative in 0.1.
